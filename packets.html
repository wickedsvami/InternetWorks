IP Addresses, Packets, and Routing

An Internet Protocol Address(IP Address), is essentially a passport for the Internet. It is a network for your computer/device that lets the Internet know where to send you your emails, data, etc. It is your digital address when you are surfing the Internet, and it follows a set standard of guidelines, hence the Protocol in IP.

The data that is sent here and there throughout the Internet is done so through packets. A packet is essentially just broken up pieces of data, which is later put back together by the computer/device that receives the packets.

These packets find their way to their destination through the process of network routing. This is done by network hardware called routers. Routers select the best and most efficient network paths for packets to travel and reach their destinations.
